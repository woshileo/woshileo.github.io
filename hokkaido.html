Leo Siow Travel Finance
October 19 — 26, 2025

Hokkaido

A week based in Sapporo at the hinge of autumn and first snow — crab feasts and soup curry, Noguchi's land-art park by bike, the Blue Pond at Biei, Moai in a cemetery, and an onsen in the Jōzankei hills.

Seven nights at the Lamp Light Books Hotel on the quiet end of the Tanukikōji arcade — a book-themed hotel with a 24-hour café, two blocks from Nijō Market in one direction and Susukino in the other. JFK to New Chitose via a long Hong Kong layover, a rental car for one big road-trip day, trains and bikes for the rest. Late October turned out to be the seam between seasons: full autumn color in the city, fresh snow on every ski hill we passed.

Day 01

Arrival into the neon

Sunday, October 19
Tanukikōji · Susukino

First wander

Landed mid-afternoon off the Hong Kong red-eye, checked into the book hotel, and went straight out under the Tanukikōji arcade — a kilometer of covered shopping street that became our spine for the week — ending at the neon wall of Susukino by the giant karaoke manekineko.

Day 02

Nijō Market & Moerenuma by bike

Monday, October 20
Nijō Market

Kaisendon breakfast at Donburi Chaya

A 6am walk through the silent city, then breakfast at the market: seafood bowls at Donburi Chaya under a ceiling of celebrity autograph boards, followed by a lap of the stalls — live crabs in crates, a fresh oyster and uni eaten standing up.

Moerenuma Park

Noguchi's park, by bicycle

Rented bikes and rode out to Moerenuma — Isamu Noguchi's last work, a whole landfill sculpted into land art. Up the long stairs of Play Mountain, around the glass pyramid, and a rainbow over the reed beds as the weather flickered between sun and squall. We had the swings to ourselves.

Tanukikōji 1 · Minami 1-jō

A two-roaster day

Mermaid Coffee Roasters in the morning (tatami benches, mermaid cups — we'd be back twice more), Baristart in the afternoon for the Hokkaidō-Jersey-milk latte and the cow-print bags. Sapporo quietly has an elite coffee scene.

Ōdōri · Susukino

TV Tower, udon, and a wagyu burger

The classic TV Tower view down Ōdōri Park, an udon lunch off Nishi 5 (tempura shrimp; niku-udon with a soft egg), jacket shopping, and dinner at Shogun Burger — wagyu patty, fried egg, no regrets — capped with Japanese natural wine at kiro., a hot-dog stand that turns into a tiny wine counter at 7pm.

Day 03

The road trip: Asahikawa → Furano → Biei

Tuesday, October 21
Asahikawa · Chuwa

cafe chater

Picked up the rental car and drove two hours north through the first snow of the season — tunnel after tunnel with white hills between. Brunch at cafe chater on Asahikawa's west side: their 10-vegetable bowls over noodles, then a soft-serve on the porch because the cone statue out front demanded it.

Kamifurano · Furano

Patchwork country

South through Kamifurano's wide-open farm panoramas, a stop at the New Furano Prince / Ningle Terrace grounds, and a Lawson pit stop in Kitanomine with fresh snow visible on the ski runs behind the parking lot. Farm Tomita was in off-season mode — crews cutting back the lavender rows — which had its own kind of honesty.

Biei · Shirogane / Daisetsuzan NP

Shirahige Falls & the Blue Pond

The day's destination: Shirahige Waterfall threading blue-white out of the rock under the bridge at Shirogane Onsen, snow on the evergreens — then ten minutes downstream, the Blue Pond itself. Milky turquoise water, bare larch trunks, autumn color on the hillside. Worth every kilometer of the drive.

Susukino · Ebi-Kani Gassen

The crab blowout

Back in Sapporo by evening for the meal the whole day had been driving toward: Ebi-Kani Gassen's twelfth-floor crab feast — a whole kegani, king crab and snow crab legs, crab sushi, chawanmushi, Sapporo Classic. The self-serve soft-serve machine at the end was the only correct dessert.

Day 04

Moai, a hidden Buddha & Moiwa at sunset

Wednesday, October 22
Makomanai Takino Cemetery

The Moai on the hill

The strangest, best detour of the trip: a working cemetery in the southern hills that commissioned Tadao Ando to bury a giant Buddha inside a lavender dome — guarded, for reasons nobody fully explains, by a row of full-size Moai. Standing under them in October mist felt like a render glitch in the best way.

Ōdōri · Minami 18-jō

Geisha coffee and shokudō ramen

Morning at Marumi Coffee's main store — two Guatemalas off the Masterpiece Selection menu, one a Cup-of-Excellence Geisha served with tasting cards — then a very different kind of excellence at a tatami-floored shokudō near the Moiwa ropeway base: old-school shōyu ramen buried in chashu.

Mt. Moiwa

Sunset over the whole city

Ropeway and then the funny little "Mōrisu Car" up the last stretch of Mt. Moiwa, timed for golden hour — the sun broke under the cloud deck right at the summit and lit the entire Sapporo basin. A late-night Hakata tonkotsu near the hotel (free kaedama, QR ordering) closed the day.

Day 05

Otaru day trip

Thursday, October 23
Sankaku Market

The kaisendon of the trip

Train along the coast to Otaru and straight into Sankaku Market, the sloped fish-stall alley hugging the station — racks of drying hokke at the top entrance and a pair of uni–ikura–crab–scallop bowls that out-ate every fancier meal of the week.

Ironai

The canal

The obligatory — and still genuinely beautiful — Otaru Canal, old stone warehouses under a brooding October sky, then umbrella weather down Sakaimachi's glassworks street with a tasting of Otaru Jōzō's gold-medal Niagara wine along the way.

Inaho

Coffee House CHAFF

A few blocks off the tourist line: a mint-green door, an old telephone booth, beans in glass jars, and patient hand-drips on robin's-egg saucers. Bought a bag of beans for home. Back in Sapporo: counter yakitori near the hotel, a basement bar with a wall of identical sculpted heads, and a peek into Booty, Susukino's no-cover funk-and-R&B disco.

Day 06

Hōheikyō Onsen & the Beer Garden

Friday, October 24
Jōzankei · Shikotsu-Tōya NP

Hōheikyō Onsen

An hour into the hills for one of Japan's largest open-air rotenburo — soaking outdoors with the whole autumn mountainside in view, plus a post-bath massage. (No photos from inside the bath, for all the right reasons.) The onsen's famous in-house Indian curry kitchen smelled incredible on the way out.

Sapporo Beer Garden

Jingisukan in Kessel Hall

Dinner under the 1912 copper brew kettle at the old red-brick brewery: jingisukan — lamb seared on dome grills — with very fresh Five Star, red-aproned staff working the hall like it's 1965. The Beer Museum facade lit up at night next door is worth the walk alone.

Day 07

Toyohira by bike & the farewell omakase

Saturday, October 25
Toyohira River · Makomanai Park

One last ride

Miyakoshiya Wiener coffee and thick toast, then bikes down the Toyohira River greenway to Makomanai Park at peak foliage — someone had lined a whole bench with collected leaves, sorted by color, and honestly it deserved a museum label.

Minami 3-jō Nishi 7

SOUL STORE

The Sapporo dish, done at one of its best addresses: soup curry under a tower of burdock chips, a dozen vegetables, rice on the side. The city's answer to ramen and arguably the better cold-weather food.

ANA Crowne Plaza B1F

Sushi-dokoro Rin

The send-off: an Edomae omakase counter hidden in a hotel basement — steamed abalone with liver sauce, then a long nigiri parade on black slate, Hokkaidō fish over red-vinegar rice. Twenty photographed courses; zero misses. Kaisen-don at the airport the next day as a final word, then home through Hong Kong.

What we ate

Hokkaido is a food destination wearing an island costume. The confirmed names below come from signage in our own photos or exact address matches; a couple of the casual spots are best guesses from where we stood.

Otaru · Sankaku Market

The kaisendon

Uni, ikura, crab, scallop and salmon in one bowl at the market by Otaru Station — the single best thing we ate all week.

Susukino · Tuesday dinner

Ebi-Kani Gassen

A whole kegani, king and snow crab legs, crab sushi and chawanmushi on the 12th floor — the full Hokkaido crab experience.

Sapporo · Saturday dinner

Sushi-dokoro Rin

Hotel-basement Edomae omakase with top-wholesaler tuna and Hokkaidō neta — the trip's blowout finale.

Sapporo · Saturday lunch

SOUL STORE

Sapporo soup curry at one of its top addresses — burdock-chip tower, a dozen vegetables, lava-warm broth.

Nijō Market · breakfasts

Market mornings

Kaisendon at Donburi Chaya, oysters and uni at the stalls, sashimi breakfasts at the Sōseigawa entrance — three mornings, zero repeats.

Sapporo Beer Garden · Friday

Jingisukan

Lamb on dome grills under the old brewery's copper kettle, washed down with the freshest Sapporo you can pour.

Coffee, everywhere

The roaster circuit

Mermaid Coffee Roasters (×3), Baristart's Jersey-milk latte, a COE Geisha at Marumi, Miyakoshiya's Wiener coffee, and CHAFF in Otaru.

Ramen, obviously

The noodle ledger

Chashu shōyu by the Moiwa ropeway, late-night Hakata tonkotsu off Tanukikōji, and udon near Ōdōri — Hokkaido carb diplomacy.
