April 16 — 20, 2026

Amsterdam

A long spring weekend in the Jordaan — canal rings and museum halls, bikes and brown cafés, and the tulip fields at the absolute peak of the season.

Four nights in an apartment on 2e Hugo de Grootstraat, right between the Jordaan and Westerpark — close enough to walk everywhere, local enough to have a corner coffee spot by day two. Overnight flight from NYC in, peak tulip season the whole time.

Day 01

Canal belt & the Rijksmuseum

Thursday, April 16
Jordaan · Grachtengordel

Straight into the canals

Off the overnight flight, bags dropped, and into the Jordaan — Lauriergracht to Keizersgracht to the Golden Bend on Herengracht, with Fabel Friet truffle-parmesan fries in hand (right at opening, before the famous line forms).

Roemer Visscherstraat

The Seven Countries Houses

One street, seven houses, seven national architectural styles — a Russian dome next to an Italian palazzo next to a Dutch gable. Worth the detour on the way to Vondelpark.

Oud-West

Vondelpark

The city's big green living room — willows over the ponds, everyone on bikes or blankets. We kept coming back: three visits in four days.

Museumplein

Rijksmuseum

An afternoon with the Dutch masters — Rembrandts and Vermeers under the cathedral-like main hall, stained glass over the grand staircase, and a giant Louise Bourgeois spider for contrast. Appeltaart and bitterballen on a canal terrace after, as is the law.

Day 02

Markets, tulips & De Wallen

Friday, April 17
Prinsengracht

Toward the Westerkerk

Iced matcha on Rozengracht, then up the Prinsengracht with the Westertoren rising over the canal — the Anne Frank House quarter, the prettiest stretch of the ring.

De Pijp

Albert Cuyp Market

The city's big street market — and the place for a stroopwafel made fresh in front of you, caramel still warm between the wafers.

Lisse · Bollenstreek

Keukenhof Gardens

Peak season at the world's most famous flower garden — seven million bulbs in bloom. The single most-photographed stop of the whole trip, and it earned it.

Heereweg, Lisse

The tulip fields

Outside the gardens, the real show: striped red and orange bulb fields running to the horizon, caught at golden hour from the road back.

De Wallen

Old town by night

After a very Dutch dinner — stamppot, biefstuk, erwtensoep — a night wander through De Wallen's lantern-lit lanes and canals.

Day 03

Jordaan markets & a bike loop

Saturday, April 18
Jordaan

Noordermarkt Saturday

Farmers' market morning at the Noordermarkt, then lunch from JWO Lekkernijen — an old-school deli, mint-green and untouched by time — eaten as baguettes on the street: filet américain on one, liver pâté on the other.

West loop

Biking like a local

Rented bikes and did the big western loop — Vondelpark, down the Schinkel, out past Overtoomse Veld and back along the canals. Red bike lanes, zero hills, total conversion to the Dutch way of life.

Brouwersgracht

Golden hour at De Belhamel

Dinner on the canal-side terrace of De Belhamel, the Art Nouveau institution at the Brouwersgracht–Herengracht junction — rack of lamb as the houseboats went gold in the evening light. Then, because Amsterdam, a second dinner: Indonesian satay on a hot stone at Long Pura near midnight.

Day 04

Windmills, cheese & a haircut

Sunday, April 19
Zaandam

Zaanse Schans

Brunch at Uncommon (their cinnamon-cardamom buns deserve their reputation), then out to the historic windmill row on the Zaan — De Kat, De Zoeker, and big Dutch skies doing exactly what the Golden Age painters promised.

De 9 Straatjes

De Kaaskamer

Cheese shopping in the Nine Streets at De Kaaskamer — floor-to-ceiling wheels of gouda and Remeker. Some of it made it home. Some of it did not survive the apartment.

Albert Cuypstraat

The traditional vacation haircut

Closed out the trip the same way as Istanbul — in a barber chair. BRUHN on Albert Cuypstraat, Sunday evening, fresh cut for the flight home. Schiphol at ten the next morning, NYC by night.

What we ate

Amsterdam fed us absurdly well — from Michelin Bib Gourmand bistros to deli baguettes on the street. A couple of the casual spots are best guesses from where we were; the dinners are all confirmed (one by a photographed receipt).

Oost · Thursday dinner

Rijsel

French-Flemish bistro, Michelin Bib Gourmand — rotisserie chicken and côte de boeuf on pewter platters. Best meal of the trip.

Leidseplein · Friday dinner

Stoop & Stoop

The full Dutch canon in one sitting: erwtensoep, bittergarnituur, biefstuk, stamppot. The €103.75 receipt made it into the photo roll.

Brouwersgracht · Saturday dinner

De Belhamel

Art Nouveau dining room, canal-side terrace, rack of lamb at golden hour. Book the outside table.

Rozengracht · Saturday, late

Long Pura

Upscale Indonesian — satay arriving on a sizzling hot stone at 11pm. Amsterdam's Indo food culture is the real deal.

Oud-West · Sunday brunch

Uncommon (Un.)

The cinnamon-cardamom buns alone justify the line. Bright plates, serious coffee.

Around the corner · Thursday

The corner coffee stop

Iced latte, cortado, and a pistachio-raspberry croissant 50 meters from the apartment — likely Cafecito on Van Oldenbarneveldtstraat.

Weteringschans · Thursday

Appeltaart & bitterballen

The mandatory Dutch terrace break after the Rijksmuseum — crispy bitterballen with mustard and a slab of apple pie.

Rozengracht · Friday

Matcha on the Rozengracht

Iced matcha and an iced strawberry matcha to start the day — likely Moychay Tea Culture Club.

Runstraat · Thursday

Fabel Friet

The famous truffle-parmesan fries, snagged at opening with zero line. Strategy matters.
