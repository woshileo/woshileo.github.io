Ringing In 2026 the Long Way

Eleven days over New Year's — temples and fight night in Bangkok, fireworks from a boat on the Chao Phraya at midnight, then waterfalls, a cooking class, and a bamboo villa in the Bali hills.

The plan was simple: spend the last days of 2025 eating through Bangkok, count down to midnight somewhere memorable, and then start the new year somewhere green. It worked out almost suspiciously well — we watched the Amazing Thailand Countdown fireworks from the deck of a boat in the middle of the Chao Phraya, and woke up (eventually) in a pool villa in Ubud. Here's the full eleven days, reconstructed photo by photo.

Chapter 01

Bangkok: the royal mile

December 28 · based off Langsuan Road, Chidlom
First afternoon · Phra Nakhon

The Grand Palace

Straight into the deep end: Wat Phra Kaew and the Grand Palace at golden hour — the gilded chedi shouldered by rows of yaksha demons, every surface either mirrored, gold, or both. December is peak season and it was still worth every elbow.

Across the river

Wat Arun, both ways

Took the cross-river ferry over to climb around Wat Arun's porcelain-studded prang up close, then rode it back at sunset with the temple doing its postcard thing behind us. Four baht, best boat ride in Asia.

Tha Tien

Wat Pho, then the crab omelette

The 46-meter Reclining Buddha at Wat Pho just before close — feet inlaid with mother-of-pearl, the hall barely big enough to hold him. Dinner two blocks away at Supanniga Eating Room: their khai yok song, a crab omelette the shape and density of a soufflé, on grandma's floral plates. We'd had Thipsamai's omelette-wrapped pad thai at Siam Paragon for lunch, so this was technically the day's second famous egg dish. No regrets.

Chapter 02

Bangkok at street level

December 29 · Lumphini → Talat Noi → Rajadamnern
7:42 AM · Lumphini Park

The vacation run

Sunrise run around Lumphini's lake with the Sathon towers coming up over the trees — the one workout of the trip that wasn't optional, because the eating schedule demanded it.

Afternoon · Talat Noi

Talat Noi

An afternoon lost in Talat Noi, the old Thai-Chinese riverside quarter — shrine alleys, engine-part shophouses, an iced tea here, a noodle bowl there. The neighborhood where Bangkok keeps its texture.

Golden hour · Yaowarat

Chinatown warm-up

Out through the Odeon Gate onto Yaowarat Road as the signs lit up — a scouting pass for the New Year's Eve return two days later. Earlier that day: durian at Terminal 21 and a coconut-ice-cream cendol in the Em district, because the eating schedule waits for no one.

7:18 PM · Rajadamnern Stadium

Fight night

Muay Thai at Rajadamnern — the world's oldest Muay Thai stadium, now rebuilt around an LED-canvas ring. An All Star Fight card, full house, gamblers signaling odds in the rafters. Last spring I trained at the camps; this time I sat in the stands and let the professionals handle it.

Chapter 03

Ayutthaya: the old capital

December 30 · day trip, 6 AM pickup
Morning · Wat Chaiwatthanaram & Wat Maha That

Coffee with a 17th-century view

Pre-dawn iced teas on the hotel walkway, a 7 AM rendezvous at River City (guarded by a five-meter inflatable Snoopy), and by 9:34 we were drinking coffee in a café that looks straight out at Wat Chaiwatthanaram's prangs. Then Wat Maha That for the image everyone comes for: the sandstone Buddha head the banyan tree decided to keep.

Midday · the royal island

Chedis and one very relaxed Buddha

Wat Phra Si Sanphet's three great chedis — the royal temple of the old kingdom — then Wat Lokaya Sutha, where a 42-meter reclining Buddha just lies out in an open field like the temple around him evaporated. Which, courtesy of the Burmese army in 1767, is exactly what happened.

Evening · Sukhumvit 33 → Langsuan

Sri Trat, then a velvet nightcap

Back in town: coffee at Bottomless, the triple-height roastery on Soi 33, then dinner up the same soi at Sri Trat — eastern-Thai cooking from Trat province, Bib Gourmand, every plate printed with the founder's mother's portrait. Grilled pork neck, Trat-style yellow curry, squid with glass noodles. Nightcap in the red-velvet bar at Velaa Sindhorn Village, where the cocktail came with an orchid and the snacks came in a Moai mug.

Chapter 04

New Year's Eve on the river

December 31 · Chidlom → Chinatown → the Chao Phraya
11:16 AM · Mercury Ville, Chidlom

The last lunch of 2025

Curry crab and a steamed whole sea bass on its little flame tray at Savoey — old-guard Bangkok seafood, eaten ten minutes from the hotel. Then a salon stop for the blowout, because New Year's Eve on a boat has a dress code if you decide it does.

Afternoon · Song Wat → ICONSIAM

Beans, then the floating market indoors

One last Chinatown pass: beans from Midsummer (盛夏), the Thai-coffee roastery in the Song Wat quarter, then across the river to ICONSIAM, where SookSiam — the indoor floating market — was in full countdown-night roar. Dinner was a progressive crawl through the stalls.

9:50 PM → midnight · the Chao Phraya

Countdown from the water

At 9:50 we boarded a countdown cruise at the ICONSIAM pier and spent the last hours of 2025 sliding up the river — past the lit-up Grand Palace, under the bridges — before anchoring off Sathon for midnight. The Amazing Thailand Countdown fireworks went off the barges right over our heads. Champagne, strangers hugging, a horn section somewhere below deck. As far as places to stand at midnight go, the middle of the Chao Phraya is going to be hard to top.

Chapter 05

Ubud: green on green

January 1 – 4 · pool villa off Jl Bisma
January 1 · 9 PM

Soft landing

New Year's Day was a travel day — Suvarnabhumi to Denpasar, then the winding drive up to Ubud. By 9 PM we were under the alang-alang roof of a pool villa in the garden belt off Jl Bisma, eating rendang off banana leaves. 2026: immediately better than advertised.

January 2 · morning

Tegalalang

First full Bali day, straight to the Tegalalang rice terraces — the great green amphitheater, palms on the ridgelines, a bamboo bridge for the obligatory photo. Got there before the heat and most of the crowd.

January 2 · afternoon

Two waterfalls, one afternoon

Kanto Lampo first — the wide cascade that fans over stepped rock, where you wade in and stand directly under it — then Tibumana, a single white plume dropping into a round pool walled with ferns. A fresh coconut between the two, machete-opened on the spot. Haircut at Barberhub and brunch at Mudra in the morning; Hujan Locale at night for sate and their braised-pork steamed buns. A heavily optimized day.

January 3 · 7 AM · Nusantara by Locavore

The cooking class, part one: the market

Up at dawn for Nusantara by Locavore's cooking class. It starts in their open kitchen, then drives north to Pasar Payangan — a real two-story village market, zero tourists — where breakfast is babi guling over rice, eaten standing between the cabbage baskets. Then a long walk through the rice paddies outside the village, foraging as you go.

January 3 · the feast

The cooking class, part two: fire

Back at the Nusantara kitchen by 11, and the next three and a half hours were the feast: whole young coconuts roasted over open flame until the water inside turns smoky, fish pepes steamed and grilled in banana-leaf parcels, sambals pounded to order. The single best food experience of the trip, and it wasn't close.

January 4 · morning

Lavender at the lotus pond

Last Ubud morning: a photoshoot at Pura Taman Saraswati in matching lavender kebaya and kamen, posing on the lotus-flower platforms over the pond — full commitment, no shame. Then the Sacred Monkey Forest, where the macaques assessed us and found us uninteresting. Lunch at a pyramid-roofed place with a raked sand garden on Jl Raya Pengosekan before the car east.

Chapter 06

Sidemen: the bamboo ending

January 4 – 6 · Avana Bamboo Villas, Selat
January 4 · arrival

The villa with the net

An hour and a half east of Ubud, the road drops into the Sidemen valley and the trip changed gears entirely: an arched, all-bamboo villa hanging over the hillside, a woven net suspended above a reflecting pond at the balcony's edge, and nothing in the view but rice terraces running down to the sea. Dinner came to the room; the robes stayed on.

January 5

A day of scheduled nothing

One full day with no itinerary, on purpose. A burger and a dragonfruit smoothie bowl at the villa restaurant, the net, a book, and a slow-motion sunset that ran the whole valley down to the coastline. The recovery day every trip claims it will have and never does — we actually had it.

January 6 · departure day

The frog and the farewell McD's

Sunrise over the valley at 6:36; at 8:20 a tree frog joined the shower, silhouetted through the curtain like a tiny security guard. Then the drive south and the airport ritual we refuse to apologize for: McDonald's Kuta Square — Indonesian-menu fried chicken, burger steak in black-pepper sauce, and a strawberry-dipped cone — plus one last white-on-white beach café on Jl Pantai Kuta before the long flight home. Wheels down in New York on the 7th, one week deep into 2026 and already behind on resolutions.

Thai-bean roastery in old Chinatown, gold letters on black — the beans that flew home with us.

The coconut bowl

Ubud · Jl Sri Wedari

Modern Indonesian: sate in curry gravy and braised-pork steamed buns out of a bamboo steamer.

The cooking class: Payangan market, a paddy walk, then fire-roasted coconuts and banana-leaf pepes. Trip MVP.

Suckling pig over rice at 8 AM, standing in a village market. Breakfast of the year, January 3rd edition.

Ubud · Jl Goutama Selatan

Paratha with raita, fried bites, and a smoothie bowl on a wooden paddle — the post-haircut brunch.

Bangkok · Langsuan

The red-velvet nightcap

Velaa Sindhorn Village's moody velvet bar — orchid-topped sour, snacks in a Moai mug. A repeat customer situation.

Kuta · the airport ritual

McDonald's Kuta Square

Indonesian McD's: fried chicken, black-pepper burger steak, strawberry-dipped cone. Tradition is tradition.
