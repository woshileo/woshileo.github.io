October 27 — November 2, 2024

Kyoto & Seoul

A week with friends through autumn-foliage Japan and into Korea — a Tokyo fashion day, the temples and vinyl bars of Kyoto, the Shinkansen, and then Seoul: a rainbow fountain on the Han and hanbok at the palace gates.

Late October is kōyō season — when the maples turn — and the plan was simple: a quick hit of Tokyo, three slow days in Kyoto, then hop the short flight to Seoul for a long weekend. Four of us, matching crewnecks, far too much shopping, and two of the great old capitals back to back.

Day 01

A Tokyo fashion day

Sunday, October 27
Shibuya & west Tokyo

Straight into the shops

One full day in Tokyo before heading west — and we spent it the way you should, working the boutiques and vintage racks of Shibuya and the western neighborhoods, bags and charms filling up by the hour.

Days 02–04

Kyoto — villas, castles & the Kamogawa

Monday–Wednesday, October 28–30
Katsura

Katsura Imperial Villa

Down on the Shinkansen and into Kyoto. A highlight came early: the reservation-only tour of Katsura Imperial Villa, the 17th-century garden estate that every modernist architect makes a pilgrimage to — numbered tour group, teahouses, and borrowed-scenery views. Kinako warabi mochi for the walk.

Kamogawa · Higashiyama

River days

Kyoto rewards walking more than ticking boxes — the Kamo River with the eastern mountains behind it, quiet residential lanes, the foliage just starting to turn on the hills.

Nijō · Arashiyama · evenings

Castles, bamboo & listening bars

The days filled with the big set pieces — Nijō Castle, the shogun's Kyoto seat with its nightingale floors, and the Arashiyama bamboo grove out west — and the evenings went to the city's vinyl listening bars and cork-ceilinged cafés, which Kyoto does better than anywhere. We left on the Hello Kitty–wrapped Haruka express for the short flight to Korea.

Days 05–07

Seoul

Thursday–Saturday, October 31 – November 2
Han River · Insadong

Rainbow fountain & ink portraits

Seoul opened at night under the Banpo Bridge Moonlight Rainbow Fountain — water and lasers arcing off the bridge into the Han. Days went to the markets and a caricature studio that turned us into ink characters.

Gyeongbokgung

Hanbok at the palace

The last day was the big one: Gyeongbokgung in rented hanbok, the throne hall of Geunjeongjeon framed dead-center through the gate, painted eaves and palace courtyards in the autumn light. (Renting hanbok also gets you in free — the city rewards playing along.)
