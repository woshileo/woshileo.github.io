May 22 — 30, 2026

Turkey

Nine days across Istanbul and Cappadocia — mosques and meyhanes, ferry rides and fairy chimneys, and one sunrise spent floating over it all.

Five nights in Istanbul, based in Galata right under the tower, then three nights in Göreme in the heart of Cappadocia. What follows is the trip day by day — the places, the food, and a few cats and dogs along the way.

Day 01 · Istanbul

Arrival in Galata

Friday, May 22
Galata · Beyoğlu

First night under the tower

Landed at Istanbul Airport in the evening and checked in a block from the Galata Tower. First impressions: the whole neighborhood is awake at midnight — cobbled lanes packed with tables, the tower glowing at the end of every street.

Day 02 · Istanbul

The Bosphorus & the Asian side

Saturday, May 23
Üsküdar · Asian shore

Beylerbeyi Palace

A boat up the Bosphorus, under the bridge, to the sultans' summer palace on the Asian shore — crystal chandeliers, painted ceilings, and a palace cat asleep on the red carpet between the marble lions.

Kadıköy

Ferry to Kadıköy

Crossed to the Asian side by ferry and wandered Kadıköy's leafy back streets — old wooden houses, street cats in every window, and a café stop by the pier.

Üsküdar

Kuzguncuk

The pastel waterfront village on the Asian shore — one long plane-tree-shaded street of bakeries, bookshop cafés, and lokantas. Stopped for a late köfte lunch.

Beşiktaş

Yıldız Park

Back on the European side, a quiet walk through Yıldız Park's mosaic paths and gardens before heading home to Galata for a late kebab dinner.

Day 03 · Istanbul

Sultanahmet

Sunday, May 24
Sultanahmet

The Blue Mosque

Sultan Ahmet Camii — six minarets and a cascade of domes. The courtyard alone is worth the visit, all grey marble and arcades under a moody sky.

Sultanahmet

Hagia Sophia

Fifteen hundred years of empire under one dome — Byzantine mosaics and Ottoman calligraphy sharing the same golden ceiling. As overwhelming as everyone says.

Sultanahmet

Topkapı Palace

Spent the whole afternoon in the Ottoman sultans' palace above Gülhane Park — the tiled corridors of the Harem, the Imperial Hall, and courtyard gardens looking out over the Bosphorus.

Somewhere in Sultanahmet

A Turkish barber detour

Mid-sightseeing haircut at a local berber — when in Istanbul. Came out sharper than any palace.

Beyoğlu

İstiklal Caddesi by night

Ended the day on İstiklal — the red nostalgic tram rolling past at dusk and a glass counter of baklava and Turkish delight that did not survive our visit.

Day 04 · Istanbul

Grand Bazaar & the Golden Horn

Monday, May 25
Fatih

The Grand Bazaar

Through the 1461 gate into the Kapalıçarşı's maze — four thousand shops of lamps, rugs, and gold under painted vaults, with the Nuruosmaniye and Bayezid mosques bookending the visit.

Karaköy

Across the Galata Bridge

Walked back over the Galata Bridge to Karaköy — fishermen on the rail, the old city skyline across the water — and into one of the best lunches of the trip.

Fatih · Golden Horn

Fener & Balat

The old Greek and Jewish quarters up the Golden Horn — candy-colored houses, rainbow stairs, and laundry lines strung between Ottoman bay windows. The most photogenic streets in the city.

Day 05 · Istanbul

Galata Tower & up the Bosphorus

Tuesday, May 26
Galata

Up the Galata Tower

Finally went up the tower we'd been sleeping next to all week. The view from the gallery sweeps the whole Golden Horn — every mosque we'd visited, laid out in one panorama.

Cihangir

Café-hopping in Cihangir

A slow afternoon in Istanbul's café neighborhood — third-wave coffee, matcha, antique shops, and the local dogs who run the place.

Ortaköy → Bebek

Up the European shore

Evening drive up the Bosphorus past the Ortaköy Mosque — white marble right on the water, framed by the bridge — then on through Arnavutköy to Bebek for a waterside fish dinner at dusk.

Day 06 · Cappadocia

Into the fairy chimneys

Wednesday, May 27
Göreme · Nevşehir

Göreme Open-Air Museum

Flew into Cappadocia at midday and went straight out — rock-cut Byzantine churches and cave dwellings carved into the cliffs, then trails through the surrounding valleys in full afternoon sun.

Göreme

Göreme by night

The village glows after dark — cave hotels lit up the hillside and fairy chimneys silhouetted behind the main street. Künefe and çay for dessert, obviously.

Day 07 · Cappadocia

The big Cappadocia day

Thursday, May 28
Göreme valleys · 6 AM

Dawn in the valleys

Up before sunrise to hike the valleys at first light. Too cloudy for the balloons to fly that morning — but we had the rose-colored canyons almost to ourselves.

Uçhisar

Uçhisar Castle

The highest point in Cappadocia — a fortress riddled with caves like a stone honeycomb. From the top, the whole valley system unrolls beneath you (castle cat included).

Derinkuyu

Derinkuyu Underground City

Eight floors straight down into the rock — an entire hidden city with stables, churches, and rolling stone doors, carved to shelter thousands. Not for the claustrophobic.

Göreme valleys · golden hour

Sunset ATV tour

Ended the day tearing through the valleys on ATVs, stopping under the fairy chimneys as the sun dropped behind Göreme.

Day 08 · Cappadocia

Ihlara Valley day trip

Friday, May 29
Aksaray

Selime Monastery

A cathedral carved entirely out of a mountain — soaring rock-cut naves with light pouring through holes in the stone. The most impressive thing we saw in Cappadocia not attached to a balloon.

Ihlara · Aksaray

Ihlara Valley

Down a few hundred wooden steps into a green river canyon — a walk along the Melendiz stream between hundred-meter cliffs, past Byzantine cave chapels cut into the walls.

Day 09 · Cappadocia

The balloon flight

Saturday, May 30
Göreme valleys · sunrise

Floating over Cappadocia

The clouds finally cleared on our last morning. Up at 5:30, in the basket by six, and floating over the fairy chimneys at sunrise with a hundred other balloons filling the sky. The single best thing we did all trip — then straight to the airport and home.

What we ate

I didn't write down every restaurant name, so a few of these are reconstructed from where we were at meal times — but the food speaks for itself.

Karaköy · Monday lunch

Karaköy Lokantası

Michelin-listed Ottoman classics — the mantı in garlicky yogurt was the dish of the trip.

Asmalımescit · Monday dinner

Meyhane night on Sofyalı Sokak

A long meze-table dinner on Beyoğlu's classic taverna street — likely Sofyalı 9 or a neighbor.

Bebek · Tuesday dinner

Fish on the Bosphorus

Grilled fish, octopus, and olive-oil meze at a waterside table in Bebek as the sun went down.

Kuzguncuk · Saturday lunch

Köfte under the plane trees

A simple lokanta lunch on Kuzguncuk's café street — köfte, salad, and fresh bread.

Kadıköy · Saturday

Kadıköy café stop

Cheesecake and iced coffee in a garden café near the ferry pier on the Asian side.

Cihangir · Tuesday

Cihangir coffee crawl

Minimalist espresso bars and an iced matcha — Istanbul's café culture more than holds its own.

Galata · Saturday late night

Late-night kebab platter

A mixed grill at a Galata spot near the hotel — proper end to a long Bosphorus day.

Galata · Friday midnight

First-night dürüm & ayran

Straight off the plane: a foil-wrapped dürüm and a cup of ayran at a counter joint. Perfect.

Göreme · most nights

Künefe & çay

Molten cheese under crisp shredded pastry and syrup, with tulip-glass tea. Ordered it more than once. No regrets.
